⚠ The Challenge

The U.S. 45Z Tax Credit Is an Export Subsidy

The U.S. Inflation Reduction Act's 45Z Clean Fuel Production Credit gives American corn ethanol producers a massive financial advantage — directly funding their ability to undercut Canadian producers in our own market.

🌽 Why It Matters

This subsidy functions as a de facto export subsidy. U.S. producers can price their ethanol aggressively in Canadian markets, making it nearly impossible for domestic Canadian producers to compete on cost alone — even with equivalent product quality and lower carbon intensity.

📊 The Ethanol Landscape

A Decade of U.S. Import Encroachment

Over the past 10 years, U.S. ethanol imports into Canada surged 150% to 3 billion litres — fuelled by Ontario's blending mandates, the Clean Fuel Regulation (CFR), and now the 45Z subsidy.

🚨 The Risk

Existential Threat to Canadian Industry

If left unaddressed, the 45Z-driven import surge poses a severe and immediate risk to the Canadian ethanol sector and the rural economy it supports.

🏭

Plant Closures

Canadian ethanol facilities cannot survive on thin margins when U.S. competitors receive a $1/gal government top-up.

🌽

Farm Revenue at Risk

Canadian ethanol plants are anchor buyers for domestic grain. Plant closures ripple directly to farmers and rural communities.

💸

Capital Flight

Investment in Canadian clean fuel infrastructure will shift to the U.S. where subsidies guarantee returns.

✅ The Solutions

Two Policy Fixes. No New Spending.

Both solutions work within Canada's existing regulatory framework — no direct government expenditure required.

01
🍁

Minimum Domestic Blending Requirement

Mandate that a minimum percentage of ethanol blended in Canada must be of Canadian origin — protecting domestic market share regardless of import pricing pressure.

Canadian Content
02
⚡

CFR Credit Multiplier for Canadian Ethanol

Amend the Clean Fuel Regulation to apply a credit multiplier to domestically produced ethanol — boosting the CFR credit value for Canadian producers to offset the 45Z subsidy advantage.

🇨🇦 The Opportunity

What We're Fighting For

These policy adjustments protect far more than an industry — they safeguard Canada's energy sovereignty, rural economy, and clean fuel future.

🛡️

Canada's Food Security Future

Ethanol production is part of Canada’s food security strategy. Only the starch in corn is used to produce ethanol, while the remaining protein, fat, and fibre are returned to the food system as high-quality animal feed. One crop produces two critical outputs: clean fuel and high-protein livestock feed.

🏭

Protect Canadian Ethanol Producers

Keep Canadian ethanol plants viable and competitive in their home market. Ensure clean fuel infrastructure investment stays north of the border

🚜

Support Rural Jobs & Farmers

Support a resilient local agricultural sector by ensuring Canadian farmers have strong, dependable domestic markets. Preserve thousands of jobs and grain procurement relationships in rural Canada.

🌿

Reduce Carbon Emissions

Canadian ethanol has lower carbon intensity — keeping it in the blend reduces GHG output.
